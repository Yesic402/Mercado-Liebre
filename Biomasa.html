Biomasa

Wood energy is the energy produced from forest biomass, in plantations dedicated to this purpose, which provide the best use of its products. After years of research and with the support of the National Council for Scientific and Technological Research (Conicit) and the Ministry of Science, Technology and Telecommunications (Micit), this initiative aims to open a new path for the country to avoid dependence on fossil fuels and move towards carbon neutrality. According to Dr. Dagoberto Arias, who leads this research at TEC, the country has climatic and geographical conditions that favor development in this field. This, added to the knowledge acquired over several years of work, has allowed to know in depth the behavior of forest species, planting in suitable sites, growth, productivity, which allow to promote innovative options to diversify the energy matrix in Costa Rican soil. In fact, other countries are already doing it, mentions Dr. Juan Pérez, researcher in forest biomass issues and expert of the University of Antioquia, in Colombia; for him there are several lines of research in the improvement of processes that allow to increase the energy potential, from that biomass that has already been previously processed.
